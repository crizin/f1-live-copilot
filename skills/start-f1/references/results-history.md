## 2025 Season

**WDC: Lando Norris (423 pts)** | **WCC: McLaren (833 pts)**

The tightest title fight in years. Norris edged Verstappen by just 2 points to claim his
first championship, ending Verstappen's four-year reign. McLaren's dominance was total —
833 points and back-to-back constructors' titles. Piastri (410 pts) was close enough to
make it a three-way battle until the final rounds.

### Key Storylines
- Norris won his first title by just 2 points — one of the closest finishes in F1 history
- McLaren's 1-2-3 in the standings (Norris-Piastri) hadn't happened since the 1988 Senna-Prost era
- Hulkenberg scored his first podium after a record 239 starts
- Antonelli impressed in his rookie season (P7, 150 pts) as Hamilton's replacement at Mercedes
- Hamilton's first season at Ferrari was underwhelming (P6, 156 pts)
- Tsunoda moved to Red Bull mid-season to replace Perez

## 2024 Season

**WDC: Max Verstappen (437 pts)** | **WCC: McLaren (666 pts)**

Verstappen claimed his 4th consecutive title but this was no cruise — McLaren surged from
mid-season to snatch the constructors' championship, their first since 1998. Six different

- McLaren's constructors' title was their first since 1998 — ended a 26-year drought

- Leclerc finally won at Monaco, his home race
- Piastri won his first race (Hungary) — McLaren's first 1-2 since 2021
- Hamilton announced his move to Ferrari for 2025

## 2023 Season

**WDC: Max Verstappen (575 pts)** | **WCC: Red Bull (860 pts)**

The most dominant season in F1 history. Verstappen won 19 of 22 races, Red Bull won 21 of 22.
The only non-Red Bull winner all year was Sainz at Singapore. Verstappen clinched his 3rd
consecutive title at the Qatar sprint — the earliest clinch by race count in the modern era.

### Key Storylines
- Verstappen's 19 wins broke the all-time single-season record (previous: Vettel 13 in 2013)
- Red Bull's 21/22 wins = 95.45% win rate, a record unlikely to ever be broken
- 10-race consecutive win streak (Miami to Italy) for Verstappen
- Singapore was the ONLY race Red Bull didn't win — Sainz broke the streak
- Alonso's resurgence at Aston Martin (8 podiums in first 8 races, then faded)
- McLaren's dramatic improvement in the second half — from backmarker to podium contender
- Piastri's impressive rookie year despite a weak early-season McLaren

## 3-Year Trends

### Driver Trajectories
- **Verstappen**: 575 → 437 → 421 pts. From total dominance to close title fights — still elite, but no longer untouchable
- **Norris**: 205 → 374 → 423 pts. Steady rise to champion. McLaren gave him the car, he delivered
- **Piastri**: 97 → 292 → 410 pts. Quiet assassin. Might be the next champion
- **Hamilton**: 234 → 223 → 156 pts. Decline continued after Ferrari move. The GOAT's twilight?
- **Leclerc**: 206 → 356 → 242 pts. Inconsistent — great 2024, disappointing 2025
- **Russell**: 175 → 245 → 319 pts. Steadily improving, Mercedes' clear #1 now
- **Antonelli**: Rookie in 2025, 150 pts — immediate impact, outscored Hamilton at Ferrari

### Team Power Shifts
- **McLaren**: 302 → 666 → 833 pts. From P4 to back-to-back champions. The 2020s dynasty?
- **Red Bull**: 860 → 589 → 451 pts. Steep decline from 2023 peak
- **Mercedes**: 409 → 468 → 469 pts. Stable but stuck in P2-P4 range
- **Ferrari**: 406 → 652 → 398 pts. Wild swings — brilliant 2024, poor 2025
