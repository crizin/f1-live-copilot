## The Big Stories

### New Regulations Shake-Up
The 2026 power unit overhaul has completely reshuffled the grid. Electric power tripled,
MGU-H gone, 100% sustainable fuel — teams that nailed the new formula are thriving,
others are struggling to adapt. The early picture:
- **Mercedes**: Got it right. Dominant in qualifying, strong in race pace.
  The new PU architecture plays to their EV expertise.
- **McLaren**: Still fast but no longer untouchable. Lost their back-to-back advantage.
- **Red Bull-Ford**: New in-house PU is a work in progress. Verstappen still extracting
  miracles, but the car isn't where it needs to be. P8 in China qualifying was a shock.
- **Audi**: First season with own PU — reliability issues but flashes of potential.
- **Cadillac**: The new 11th team. Backmarker for now, but just being on the grid is
  the achievement. Bottas + Perez bringing experience.

### Antonelli's Breakout
Kimi Antonelli, just 20, is leading the World Championship. After a solid rookie year
in 2025 (P7, 150 pts), he's taken a massive step. Pole position and podium in China
had the paddock buzzing. Is this the next great champion, or an early-season mirage?

### Bahrain & Saudi Arabia Called Off
Both Middle Eastern rounds were cancelled — a first for the modern calendar. Geopolitical
tensions forced the FIA's hand. The 2026 calendar is now 22 races instead of 24.
Debate rages about whether the sport is too dependent on Middle Eastern venues.

### Hamilton's Ferrari Struggles (Year 2)
After a disappointing first season at Ferrari (P6, 156 pts), pressure is mounting.
The 7x champion is now 41 and being matched or beaten by Leclerc. Is this the farewell
tour, or can the new regs give him a second wind? Early signs are mixed — P6/P2/P6
in the first three races.

### Active Aero Era Begins
The new active aerodynamics rules have added a fascinating strategic layer. Teams can
adjust aero surfaces on the fly — think of it as DRS on steroids, but with defensive
and cornering applications too. It's changed overtaking dynamics significantly.
Some drivers have adapted faster than others.

## Rivalries to Watch

- **Antonelli vs Russell** (Mercedes): The student threatening to surpass the master.
  Russell was "Mr. Saturday" but Antonelli is taking poles from him.
- **Norris vs Verstappen**: 2025 title rivals, now in different tiers of machinery.
  Can Verstappen do it with an inferior car like 2024?
- **Hamilton vs Leclerc** (Ferrari): The GOAT and the heir. Second season together,
  team dynamics getting tense.
